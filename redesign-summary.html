In Detail

The seven changes that matter most

01

A live map of real project sites

The centrepiece of the new site. All 16 projects from the Company Profile — including the Lotsane and Gaborone Dam LiDAR surveys currently underway — are plotted on a satellite map, filterable by service type. This is the single strongest way to show a visiting client the scale and spread of the work without them reading a PDF.

See the map →
02

Partner stories, not just a logo wall

Geoflux, Lucara, Champs Botswana, Urban Heights, Envirosolve and Wild African Safaris each get a short card describing the actual work done together. Seven more partners (Trail Surveys, Stet Surveying, Geosurv, Asphalt, J.S Planning, Integrated Engineers, Ministry of Health) show their project on hover or keyboard focus, so the page stays uncluttered but the detail is one interaction away.

See the partner stories →
03

Brand colours pulled from the real logo

The whole site is re-themed in the green and white sampled directly from the Ultimatum Finesse logo, rather than a generic template palette. Every page, button and map marker uses the same system.

See the homepage →
04

Handheld LiDAR Stockpile Scanning added

Added as service #7, exactly as requested — walk-around handheld scanning for fast, repeatable stockpile volume measurements at mines, quarries and aggregate yards.

See the service page →
05

An Insights page that replaces the empty blog

The old blog had one default WordPress post and nothing since. Insights is a short, curated reading list — one real article per service line, from sources like Esri, Autodesk and FARO — written for the people commissioning the work, not the people doing it. Each has a real photo relevant to the topic.

See Insights →
06

The Company Profile is finally on the website

Previously the profile only existed as a PDF sent by email. It's now hosted on the site and downloadable from a prominent button at the top of the About page — one of the first things a visitor sees.

See the About page →
07

Built to modern web standards

Keyboard navigation, screen-reader support, visible focus states, and a "skip to content" link on every page. Images are sized to prevent layout jumping while the page loads. Tested at phone, tablet and desktop widths.

Unchanged

What we kept

The redesign is a rebuild of the presentation, not a rewrite of the company. Every fact below is carried over unchanged from your Company Profile and original site.

✓ Founded 2013, proudly citizen-owned, CAAB-certified for commercial drone operations
✓ All seven — now — service disciplines and their real applications
✓ Office address, phone lines and email exactly as published
✓ Vision and Mission statements, word for word
✓ Every client and partner named in the Company Profile
